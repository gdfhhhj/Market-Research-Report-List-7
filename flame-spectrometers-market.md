<p><p>Flame spectrometers analyze elements by measuring light emitted from excited atoms in a flame. The **UV-Visible Spectrometer** detects elements using ultraviolet and visible light absorption, ideal for metals and compounds with specific electronic transitions. The **IR Spectrometer** identifies molecular vibrations in the infrared range, suited for organic compounds and functional groups. Both types are widely used in industries like pharmaceuticals, environmental testing, and material analysis, offering precise, non-destructive elemental and molecular identification. Their applications depend on the wavelength range and sample properties.</p></p>
