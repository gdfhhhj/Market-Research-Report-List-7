<p><p>Vehicle-To-Vehicle (V2V) communication encompasses technologies that allow vehicles to exchange information, enhancing road safety and traffic efficiency. Two primary market types are Cellular-Based Technology and Dedicated Short-Range Communications (DSRC). Cellular-Based Technology leverages existing cellular networks for broader coverage and higher data rates, enabling real-time communication over longer distances. In contrast, DSRC is a short-range, high-speed wireless protocol specifically designed for automotive applications, facilitating low-latency exchanges of safety-critical information between nearby vehicles. Both technologies aim to improve vehicular communication.</p></p>
