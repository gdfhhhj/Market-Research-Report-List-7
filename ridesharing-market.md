<p><p>Ridesharing markets can be categorized into three types: </p><p>1. **Commuting Ridesharing** focuses on shared travel for daily commutes, helping individuals reach work or school efficiently while reducing traffic congestion. </p><p>2. **Dynamic Ridesharing** adjusts in real-time based on demand, allowing users to request rides as needed, often utilizing algorithms to match passengers with drivers. </p><p>3. **Fixed Long Distance Ridesharing** involves organized sharing for longer journeys between cities, where travelers coordinate to split costs, making it economical for both drivers and passengers.</p></p>
